App Review Analysis · APM Proof-of-Work · Product Strategy
What do 160 Play Store reviews reveal about Clinikally's biggest operational risk?
An end-to-end APM proof-of-work for Clinikally (YC S22) — individually reading and coding every review to surface three post-purchase failures, a review integrity anomaly, and four prioritised fixes.
Problem
Clinikally's 98.1% positive Play Store rating masked three critical post-purchase failures — none with a visible resolution path for the customer. One involved prescription medication.
Approach
Read all 160 reviews individually, mapped complaints and feature themes, flagged a suspicious single-day volume spike (150 of 160 reviews on one date), and built evidence-backed recommendations for the APM role.
Business Impact
Identified prescription non-fulfillment as a health risk (not logistics), flagged probable Google Play policy violations from review-gating, and ranked 4 product fixes from P0 to P2 by severity and effort.
Key Insights
1
Shared root cause across all 3 complaints
Post-purchase operations are broken. No functioning support line, no proactive delivery communication, no return escalation path — the failure starts the moment an order leaves the cart.
2
Concern-based browsing is the moat — underutilised
157 positive reviews mapped across 9 feature themes. Concern browsing led at 28 mentions. Clara (AI routine-builder) was surfaced once and deserves a bigger surface — it's Clinikally's clearest differentiator.
3
Review integrity flag — proactive risk identification
~150 of 160 reviews dated to a single day. Gaming-adjacent usernames, near-identical phrasing. Flagged as probable incentivised review push in violation of Google Play Developer Policy.

Customer Intelligence · Review Mining · Brand Strategy
The product is liked. The positioning is confusing the wrong customers away.
A structured 4-tab customer intelligence brief for Toyo Kombucha — Amazon India review analysis revealing a core positioning split between two incompatible customer segments, with 5 ranked recommendations.
Problem
~30% of Toyo's Amazon reviews questioned whether it's "real kombucha" — a conversion-killing authenticity debate playing out publicly, with no pre-emptive response from the brand's listing or website copy.
Approach
Analysed Amazon India reviews, mapped friction by flavour SKU, traced sugar messaging gaps, and built a 4-tab interactive brief covering what's working, what's creating friction, and 5 evidence-backed recommendations.
Business Impact
Identified two incompatible customer segments sharing one positioning — and that a messaging fix (not a product change) resolves the conversion problem. Each rec includes a KPI to track and implementation effort rating.
Key Insights
1
Two customers, one product, zero segment differentiation
Casual health drinkers (taste-positive, repeat buyers) and kombucha purists (SCOBY-seekers, acidity-focused) are served by the same positioning. The product works for group 1; the brand tries to serve both and fully convinces neither.
2
Taste and customer service are genuine moats — not leveraged
90% positive taste mentions. A highly-upvoted review called Toyo's post-purchase handling benchmark D2C service. Neither asset is institutionalised as a KPI or foregrounded in brand messaging.
3
Three friction points compounding quietly
Authenticity debate (conversion risk), sugar messaging confusion across SKUs (retention risk), and a cap-seal packaging QC issue (safety risk at scale). Each requires a different fix — operations, copy, and production.

Customer Intelligence · Sentiment Analysis · Product Strategy
What are Zouk's customers actually trying to tell them?
Built a full customer intelligence dashboard analyzing 98 app reviews, mining complaint patterns, tracking sentiment over time, and turning raw feedback into 5 prioritized product recommendations for a premium D2C accessories brand.
Problem
Zouk had thousands of reviews with no structured way to identify which issues mattered most, how sentiment was trending, or what to fix first.
Approach
Read all 98 reviews individually, coded by sentiment and issue category, built a pareto chart of complaints, and designed an interactive 4-tab dashboard. Not a summary but a working tool.
Business Impact
5 evidence-backed recommendations ranked by impact and effort, each anchored to specific review evidence, including a paid expedited delivery tier issue creating legal exposure.
Key Insights
1
Delivery delay is the dominant complaint, and silence amplifies it
Delivery issues appeared in 39.6% of negative reviews and jumped to 50% in Apr-Jun. Almost every case involved no proactive communication. Customers only got answers after escalating publicly.
2
Support responsiveness co-occurs in 41.7% of negative reviews
A 98% public reply rate masked the fact that replies redirected to one inbox multiple reviewers said went unanswered. The metric looked good. The experience wasn't.
3
Paid expedited delivery is a trust liability and legal risk
Multiple reviewers paid for 1-2 day delivery and waited 7-11+ days. Charging for a service not delivered is a revenue integrity and regulatory exposure problem.

Why do agri-commodity prices behave unpredictably near MSP floors?
Built a 3-page interactive intelligence dashboard tracking commodity prices, market arrivals, MSP risk, and 30-day price forecasts across Indian commodity markets.
Problem
Traders, FPOs, and agri-enterprises had no consolidated view of commodity price movements relative to MSP, forcing reactive rather than strategic selling decisions.

Customer Research
The most useful research happens before you open the spreadsheet. During field research across Rajasthan, I found that farmers adopt new seeds based on what a neighbour tried last season, not yield claims. Quantitative data tells you what happened. Field conversations tell you why.

Operations
The most valuable operational insight at Sarvagram wasn't in any report. Collection delays weren't a credit issue; they were a timing issue. No one assigned me to figure that out. I built the MIS system to see it, then fixed it. Systems reveal what intuition misses.
📊
Analytics
Analytics without context is just noise. The Zouk case study showed me that a 98% public reply rate can mask a support inbox nobody reads. The number looked healthy. The experience wasn't. Good analysis asks what the metric isn't telling you.
